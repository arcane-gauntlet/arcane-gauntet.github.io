Type Chart Changes

It is no secret that in Pokémon, not all types are created equal. However, in our quest to allow you to basically use any Pokémon you want, our goal is going to be to help struggling types whilst maintaining the general look and feel of how types interact with each other.

These changes were inspired by the highly unique Ultra Sun ROM hack Pokémon Sweltering Sun by Dooz!

Ice​

Ice now resists Ground, Flying and Dragon

Throughout Pokémon's history, the Ice type has been known as a highly dominant offensive type, creating monstrous threats such as Chien-Pao, Darmanitan-Galar and Kyurem-White. However, in equal quantities, there are Ice-types that simply do not work because they are slow and bulky Pokémon intended to act as walls, but with no resistances except Ice itself, to balance out their common weaknesses, leading them to be worn down throughout the course of a match - see: Avalugg, Regice and Walrein. Thus, by flipping some of Ice's offensive interactions into resistances, defensive Ice-types like will now be far more viable - in addition, this will help to aid the ability of some frailer Ice-types like Weavile to switch in.

Bug​

Fighting, Ghost and Fairy no longer resist Bug

Bug is an example of a type which does not have a strong offensive OR defensive presence. These are the main three types that don't really make a lot of sense lore-wise to resist Bug - and it would also be of great benefit to particular Bug types if it did not have to carry coverage for them.
